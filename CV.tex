\section{Personal Profile}
Theoretical condensed matter physicist completing a PhD (expected late 2026/early 2027) at the \href{https://sites.google.com/view/qudyma-icmm}{QUDYMA} and \href{https://wp.icmm.csic.es/tqe/}{Q4Q} groups at \href{https://www.icmm.csic.es/}{ICMM}-\href{https://www.csic.es/en}{CSIC}, Madrid, under \href{https://elsaprada.github.io/}{Elsa Prada} and \href{https://wp.icmm.csic.es/tqe/people/ramon-aguado/}{Ramón Aguado}. My research focuses on topological superconductivity and Majorana physics in hybrid semiconductor-superconductor nanowires, combining large-scale numerical simulations with analytical modelling to address open questions relevant to topological quantum computing. I have published eight papers in peer-reviewed journals, with first or co-first authorship on six of them, and have additional ongoing collaborations with the experimental groups of Eduardo Lee and \href{https://nbi.ku.dk/english/staff/?pure=en/persons/67039}{Jesper Nygård}. My work has been developed in close collaboration with leading experimental groups, including those of \href{https://marcuslab.harvard.edu/}{C.~M.~Marcus} and \href{https://www.nbi.ku.dk/english/research/condensed-matter-physics/condensed-matter-theory/vaitiekenas-group/}{S.~Vaitiekėnas}. I also undertook a research visit to \href{https://nbi.ku.dk/english/research/condensed-matter-physics/condensed-matter-theory/}{Karsten Flensberg's group} at the Niels Bohr Institute (April–July 2025), where I initiated a new research direction on non-Hermitian transport in hybrid devices.
